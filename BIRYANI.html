BIRYANI FROM WAHID & IDRIS

There are some people who become instantly happy just thinking about biryani and if you are one of them or even if you are someone who likes to eat a good plate of biryani, Lucknow is going to make you super happy. Because biriyani is a Lucknow food speciality. Long grains of rice, with marinated meat and some selected spices, are kept on dum for a long time and then served with raita and sliced onions. You can have a heart-filling plate of biryani in many food joints in Lucknow. But we do have two suggestions. Try biryani from Idris. The aroma and the flavours are to die for. And to taste the best biriyani from the Awadhi Lucknow cuisine, you have to visit Wahid’s.

The biryani from Idrees is indeed very nice but we usually pack it and bring it home and relish it. It’s not a comfortable place to sit and eat at after all,” said the young lady from the Renaissance Hotel in Lucknow where I had stayed during my last trip to the city. She was a local. Loved her city and its food. She was happy to see my enthusiasm to check out some of the local gems of the city. Yet, when it came to the Idrees Hotel at Lucknow, she seemed hesitant. Well, let me explain why I was so keen to go to Idrees.

In my previous trip to Lucknow, I had gone on what I called a ‘biryani pilgrimage’. I had read that the biryani of Kolkata owes its existence to Lucknow and specifically to Nawab Wajid Ali Shah’s moving to Kolkata from Lucknow. I was keen to go to Lucknow and taste the biryani that is supposed to be the forefather of the biryanis of the Shiraz’s and Nizam’s in Kolkata that I grew up on and so love. A work trip to Lucknow came up then fortuitously and I set off on the biryani trail.
